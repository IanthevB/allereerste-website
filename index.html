Praise Pytheas!

This website is still under construction; spelling errors are still present, and content is still lacking. In time, information will be added and corrected.

This website will be a micro-history. It will concern the live and job of a man who lived in the fourth century BC. His name was: Pytheas of Alopeke. In this Website we shall have a look into his daily life during the year 333/332 BC.

We shall explore his life through an inscription: an honorary decree made in the year 333/332 BC for his services, and lists his actions and rewards. He was honored with a crown for building fountains and taking care of the water supply for an imporant healing sanctuary in Greece: the Amphiareion.

What might first appear as a simple rock with soms scribblings on it: becomes an amazing source of information, which deserves to be studied properly and precisely.

By specifically zooming in on this inscription we will not only dive in to the life of this ordinary, but important ancient Greek citizen, but will also encounter the amazing possibilities the study of inscriptions, epigraphy, has for accessing the ancient world.

Since it was found, this inscription has been a major source of information for many different aspects of the ancient world. On this website, this inscriptions is completely documentented, annotated and explained.

This website contains both visual, audio and articles which portray, discuss, illustrate and explore this inscription.

Through these several interactive options, this website hopes to help the reader understand the value of epigraphy as an ancient source. It illustrates how we can study such an inscription, and how many information (on all sorts of topics) we can extract from it.
